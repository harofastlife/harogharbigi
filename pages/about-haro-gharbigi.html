Things Named Haro (That Aren't Me)

Herbig-Haro Objects

Bright patches of nebulosity associated with newborn stars. They form when narrow jets of partially ionised gas ejected by stars collide with nearby clouds of gas and dust at several hundred kilometers per second. Named after astronomers George Herbig and Guillermo Haro who first studied them in detail in the 1940s.
